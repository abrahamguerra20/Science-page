Characterization and Pangenomics of *Halobacterium* from an Unexplored Saltern

This study reports the first isolation of a carotenoid-producing halophilic archaeon, strain oMaY14, from the Galerazamba Solar Saltern in Colombia. Identified as a member of the *Halobacterium* genus, this isolate produces bacterioruberin in high-salt conditions. To place this discovery in a broader context, a comprehensive phylo-pangenomic analysis of the *Halobacterium* genus was conducted using 47 genomes. The analysis revealed an open pangenome, indicating significant genomic diversity, and allowed for a precise taxonomic re-evaluation using ANI, leading to proposed reclassifications within the genus. This work combines novel isolation with large-scale comparative genomics to expand our understanding of these extremophiles.
