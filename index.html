Research Projects

Comparative genomics of the nitroplast

The diazotroph *Candidatus Atelocyanobacterium thalassae* (UCYN-A) has evolved into a nitrogen-fixing organelle, the nitroplast. Our pangenome analysis revealed extensive metabolic reduction and a previously unrecognized divergence within the genus, leading us to propose two distinct species: *Ca. A. thalassae* and *Ca. A. braarudosphaerae sp. nov.*

Revealing Two Species within *Methanobrevibacter smithii*

This study moves beyond 16S rRNA by using comparative genomics and ANI analysis on 94 genomes to reclassify *M. smithii*. We uncovered significant divergence, leading to its reclassification into two species: *M. smithii* and *Candidatus M. intestini*. We also identified genes related to antibiotic resistance and pathogenicity.

Genomics of *Lactiplantibacillus plantarum* HCA1

This study examines HCA1, a lactic acid bacterium from pig manure. Its 3.28 Mb genome contains multiple plantaricin genes for antimicrobial activity and genes for acid/bile tolerance, supporting its probiotic potential. Its open pangenome suggests genomic plasticity, making it a strong candidate for improving gut health and food safety.

Pangenomics of *Candidatus Liberibacter asiaticus* (CLas)

This research involves a deep dive into the pangenome of *Ca. L. asiaticus*, the causative agent of citrus greening disease. The goal is to identify core genomic features, diversity, and virulence factors to inform future control strategies for this devastating agricultural pathogen.

Population Genetics of Aquatic Carnivorous Plants

This genomic study investigated the genetic diversity of the carnivorous plants *Utricularia foliosa* and *U. gibba* in Brazil and Colombia. Using Genotype-by-Sequencing (GBS), our analysis revealed geographic clustering and complex genetic structuring, highlighting significant diversity and long-distance, avian-mediated gene flow.

Microbial Diversity in a Mexican Cloud Forest

This study evaluated the bacterial and fungal communities in a pristine montane cloud forest in Oaxaca. We revealed a high prevalence of entomopathogenic fungi (like *Tolypocladium*) and potential phosphate-solubilizing bacteria (*Pseudomonas* and *Bacillus*), highlighting the need for in-situ conservation.

Usiacurí Mineral Water Wells

This project assessed the environmental potential and health risks of mineral water wells in Usiacurí, Colombia. It involved investigating their microbiological and chemical composition to identify contaminants like pathogens and toxic substances, contributing to policies for safe water supply.

Propagation of Ornamental Plants

This project focused on optimizing growth and reproduction methods for ornamental plants and succulents. We explored techniques like cuttings, grafting, and seed propagation to develop efficient and sustainable practices for the horticultural industry.

Review Articles

The Pangenome: A Statistical Model

This review provides an overview of the pangenomic landscape, emphasizing that the pangenome is a statistical model dependent on dataset quality and taxonomic resolution. We discuss the transition to nuanced, ecologically-driven models and highlight unique challenges and insights from the under-explored archaeal pangenome.

Human-Associated Archaea: A Neglected Microbiome

Archaea colonize various human body sites, yet the archaeome remains poorly studied despite its potential role in health and disease. This review covers their transmission, involvement in conditions like periodontitis, and the need for further studies to understand their precise mechanisms and impact.

The Genus *Utricularia* in Colombia

These carnivorous plants are indicators of water quality and hold biotechnological potential. This review analyzes the state of knowledge of the 41 *Utricularia* species in Colombia, highlighting the need for more research into their genomics, ecological roles, and potential for nature-based solutions.
