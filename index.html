Comprehensive Analysis of the *Halobacterium* Genus through Phylo-pangenomic Approaches

This study performed a comprehensive phylo-pangenomic analysis of the Halobacterium genus using 47 available genomes. The analysis revealed an open pangenome, suggesting high genomic diversity. The phylogenomic analysis, coupled with Average Nucleotide Identity (ANI), allowed for a precise taxonomic re-evaluation, leading to the proposed reclassification of several strains. These findings underscore the importance of genomic approaches for robust and accurate microbial classification.

Isolation and Characterization of Halophilic Archaea from the Galerazamba Solar Saltern

This study reports the first isolation of a halophilic archaeon from the Galerazamba salterns in Colombia. A strain, oMaY14, was identified as *Halobacterium*. It grows in high salt concentrations (15-25%) and produces a pink-reddish pigmentation. Analysis via UV-visible spectrophotometry and Raman spectroscopy confirmed the presence of bacterioruberin. This work describes a novel isolate with significant potential for producing industrially relevant carotenoids.
