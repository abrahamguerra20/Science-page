Abraham David Guerra Ospino

Microbiologist | Bioinformatician | Researcher in Genetics & Biotechnology

My research focuses on Microbiomes, Pangenomics, and Metagenomics, with an emphasis on Comparative Genomics, Extremophiles, Archaea, and Plant-Associated Microbiomes.

About Me

I am a Microbiologist with a Bachelor's degree from Universidad Simón Bolívar. Currently, I am a CAPES scholarship holder pursuing a Master’s degree in Plant Genetics and Breeding at São Paulo State University (UNESP). I am also an active researcher at CBioClima - Center for Research in Biodiversity and Climate Change. My work integrates genomics, bioinformatics, and molecular biology to explore microbial communities, with the goal of developing innovative and sustainable biotechnological solutions.

Featured Projects

Pangenome of 'Candidatus Liberibacter asiaticus' (CLas)

Unraveling the genomic diversity of the bacterium causing citrus greening (HLB). This project aims to identify virulence factors and guide the development of new control strategies.

Rethinking Methanobrevibacter smithii

Using comparative genomics to reveal that this human-associated archaeon comprises two distinct species, challenging traditional classification and opening new questions about its role in health.

Probiotic Potential of Lactiplantibacillus plantarum HCA1

Genomic characterization of a bacterial strain with high probiotic and antimicrobial potential, positioning it as a promising candidate for health and food preservation applications.

Genetics of Aquatic Carnivorous Plants

A genomic study of *Utricularia* species in Colombia and Brazil to understand their diversity and evolution, revealing long-distance gene flow patterns, possibly mediated by birds.

Usiacurí Mineral Water Wells

Assessed the environmental potential and health risks of mineral water wells by investigating their microbiological and chemical composition to identify potential contaminants.

Microbial Diversity in a Mexican Cloud Forest

Characterized the bacterial and fungal diversity in an undisturbed montane cloud forest in Oaxaca, Mexico, revealing a high prevalence of key functional species like phosphate solubilizers.

Propagation of Ornamental Plants

Focused on optimizing growth and reproduction methods for ornamental plants and succulents, exploring techniques like cuttings, grafting, and seed propagation to improve yield and quality.
